The iPhone X, released in November 2017, marked a significant milestone for Apple, commemorating the 10th anniversary of the iPhone. This model introduced a dramatic redesign with a nearly bezel-less, edge-to-edge 5.8-inch Super Retina OLED display, eliminating the iconic Home button. Instead, it featured Face ID, a revolutionary facial recognition technology for secure authentication, made possible by the TrueDepth camera system. The iPhone X was powered by the A11 Bionic chip, offering superior performance and efficiency. It also boasted dual 12MP rear cameras with advanced optical image stabilization and a 7MP front TrueDepth camera, enabling innovative features like Animoji. With its glass back and stainless steel frame, the iPhone X supported wireless charging and offered water and dust resistance rated at IP67. This model set the stage for future iPhones, emphasizing a sleek design, advanced biometric security, and cutting-edge technology.

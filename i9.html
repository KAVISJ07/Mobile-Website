There is no iPhone 9. After the release of the iPhone 8 and iPhone 8 Plus in September 2017, Apple skipped directly to the iPhone X (pronounced "ten") to celebrate the 10th anniversary of the iPhone. Possible Reasons for Skipping iPhone 9 1. Marketing Strategy: The jump from iPhone 8 to iPhone X (ten) signified a major leap in technology and design, marking the 10th anniversary of the iPhone. 2. Technological Leap: The iPhone X introduced several groundbreaking features, such as: - Edge-to-Edge Display: A nearly bezel-less OLED screen. - Face ID: Advanced facial recognition technology replacing Touch ID. - Removal of Home Button: A new navigation system using gestures. - New Design: A more modern and premium design with stainless steel and glass. Evolution After iPhone 8 - iPhone X: Released in November 2017, featuring a 5.8-inch Super Retina display, Face ID, and a new design. - iPhone XS and XS Max: Released in September 2018, with improvements in performance, camera, and a larger display for the XS Max. - iPhone XR: Also released in September 2018, offering a more affordable option with an LCD display and a variety of colors. - iPhone 11 Series: Released in September 2019, with significant improvements in cameras, battery life, and performance. By skipping the iPhone 9, Apple aimed to highlight the significant advancements and create a clear distinction between the older models and the innovative features introduced with the iPhone X and subsequent models.

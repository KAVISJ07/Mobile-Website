The iPhone 12, released in October 2020, represented a significant leap forward for Apple's smartphone lineup, introducing several new features and design changes. With its sleek, flat-edged design reminiscent of the iPhone 4, the iPhone 12 combined aesthetics with functionality. It featured a 6.1-inch Super Retina XDR display, providing vibrant colors and deep blacks for an immersive viewing experience. One of the most notable upgrades was the inclusion of 5G connectivity, enabling faster internet speeds and improved performance in crowded areas. The device was powered by the A14 Bionic chip, the first built on a 5-nanometer process, delivering unprecedented performance and energy efficiency. The dual-camera system, consisting of 12MP wide and ultra-wide lenses, offered impressive photo and video capabilities, enhanced by Night mode and Deep Fusion technology for better low-light photography. The front Ceramic Shield provided four times better drop performance, significantly improving durability. Additionally, the iPhone 12 introduced MagSafe, a new magnetic attachment system that allowed for easy and secure accessory attachment and more efficient wireless charging. With its advanced features and refined design, the iPhone 12 set a new standard for what users could expect from a smartphone.
